## Design Context

### Users
Minecraft creators, video thumbnail makers, server staff, skin artists, and modpack/community organizers who need fast character renders for posts, thumbnails, profile art, and project pages.

### Use Cases
Load a player skin or uploaded PNG, pose the character, adjust lighting and visual treatment, compose a creator-friendly background or thumbnail, and export an image that can drop straight into an editor.

### Brand Personality
Practical, maker-focused, and precise with a little creator-studio energy. The app should feel like a capable workshop instrument for Minecraft media rather than a toy or a marketing page.

### Aesthetic Direction
Dense utility webapp with a large first-screen 3D viewport, compact controls, readable surfaces, restrained blue/teal accents, and Minecraft-grounded material cues. It should feel related to `schematic-editor`: calm, modern, and immediately usable.

### Design Principles
- Put the posed skin at the center of the first viewport.
- Keep controls compact, predictable, and reachable while editing.
- Use color for active state, export confidence, and creator-grade emphasis.
- Make empty and loading states actionable without long instructions.
- Prefer performance, clarity, and reliable export behavior over ornamental UI.
